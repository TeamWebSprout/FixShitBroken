The "Protecting American Prosperity Act"

H.R. 4471 · 1,247 pages · Sponsored by Rep. M. Hollis (R-FL) · 84 cosponsors
An omnibus tax-and-spending package that bundles four largely unrelated provisions: a corporate tax cut extension, a SNAP eligibility tightening, an energy industry subsidy, and a pharmaceutical patent cap increase. Backers describe it as a "competitiveness package." Opponents call it the most direct corporate handout of the year.

The 30-second summary

1,247 pages·30 sec read·Last updated 2hr ago

This is four separate bills duct-taped together and given a feel-good name. Strip away the patriotic packaging and you get: extend an offshore corporate tax cut, cut SNAP eligibility, hand $48B to four energy companies, and let pharma keep patents longer.

The legislation is being moved fast — full House vote in 3 days — which is unusually quick for a 1,247-page omnibus. Senate companion bill not yet introduced. Expected to pass the House on party lines; faces tougher math in Senate.

What it actually does

  • Extends the 2018 offshore corporate income tax reduction through 2032, costing approximately $94B over 10 years per the CBO score.
  • Cuts SNAP eligibility for households earning over $24,000 by adding a stricter asset test. Approximately 1.4M households would lose benefits.
  • Authorizes a $48B subsidy split among four specifically-named energy companies for "domestic infrastructure modernization."
  • Raises the federal cap on pharmaceutical patent extensions from 5 to 7 years, delaying generic competition.
  • Reorganizes three small federal agencies under a new "Office of American Prosperity" with expanded rulemaking authority.
